Jump to Finding Evolution Cases Formula Operating engine Implications 3 angles Mechanic Mars handoff

The finding. Lifetime value as an enterprise operating model has been built most completely in healthcare — and within healthcare, in integrated payers and value-based care rather than pharma. The strongest published case is Humana's Medicare Advantage operating model.

Humana operates explicit CLTV and NPV per member, using both to allocate acquisition spend, design retention programmes, and price products to sustainable margin. Patients in its value-based care arrangements show 24.3% lower inpatient admissions, 13.4% fewer ED visits, and an estimated $12.8B in savings versus traditional Medicare. The transferable lesson is the connection between the formula and day-to-day operating decisions.

AstraZeneca provides the predictive lens. Its MILTON model predicts disease risk up to 15 years before clinical diagnosis across 1,000+ conditions, integrating de-identified records with genetic and protein data. Pharma does not publish full CLTV operating models the way payers do, but the early-intervention logic is directly transferable to senior-pet risk scoring.

Trupanion provides the formula discipline at the pet level. Explicit profit CLTV per pet, PAC ~$315, monthly retention 98.25–98.4%, with acquisition spend adjusted in near-real time against cohort IRR. Trupanion is an insurance model — a contractual single-product structure — which does not translate to a multi-division ecosystem.

Translated for Mars. Keep divisional P&Ls intact. Build a shadow value ledger. Risk-stratify the senior-pet cohort. Run test/control with pre-registered outcomes. Use leading indicators (adherence, screening uptake) to inform forward LTV. Defensibility comes from the operating rhythm, which is harder to replicate than the model itself.

The executive finding

Healthcare LTV only works when the formula changes the operating model.

The strongest published parallel is integrated healthcare and payer value-based care, rather than pharma. In these models, lifetime value, prevention, retention, care quality and cost-to-serve are wired into day-to-day operating decisions.

The same shift has played out in pharma and healthcare over the last decade.

Industry-wide, the operating metric has migrated from product volume to longitudinal value tied to outcomes, retention and ecosystem economics. Four operating principles

These are the design choices that, in our experience, materially affect whether health-economics models reach scale.

PRINCIPLE 01

Anchor in MVH — depth, frequency, first-party data

MVH is the only Mars asset with clinical depth, identity resolution and recurring touchpoints in the same place. Start the value tree where the cause-and-effect logic is cleanest. Let signals flow outward to Royal Canin, Pet Nutrition and partners.

PRINCIPLE 02

Run backstage, not frontstage

Divisions keep their brands and P&Ls. The ecosystem orchestrates behind the scenes — a data spine, a shared signal model, a value ledger. No consumer-facing brand consolidation, no organisational reset.

PRINCIPLE 03

Incentives travel with the journey, not the division

Previous OGSMs failed on mismatched incentives — the President's own diagnosis. A cross-divisional value ledger is the smallest mechanism that rewards enterprise value without breaking divisional accountability. Design it before scaling.

PRINCIPLE 04

Build the minimum signal set, not the data lake

The signal set sufficient to trigger ecosystem action is small. A unified 360 view is necessary eventually, but not first — building the data architecture ahead of the value moments turns data itself into the work.

05 · Enablers

People, process, tech — what has to be true for this to work.

The pilot produces the result. These three layers — people, process and technology — are what allow that result to scale. None of them needs to be complete at the start; each requires a defensible v0 by the time the pilot reports, so the scale decision can be made against real evidence.

● PEOPLE

A small core team, healthcare-fluent

The President's leadership team is drawn largely from healthcare — a structural advantage when bringing health-economics methods inside.

  • 01
    Executive sponsorFrom President's office — owns the agenda, unblocks division friction, signs the framing memo.
  • 02
    MVH clinical leadProtects clinical integrity, owns the workflow design, signs off on pre-registered outcomes.
  • 03
    Cross-divisional ops leadSits across Royal Canin + Antech + MVH — designs the joined-up pathway, owns delivery.
  • 04
    Data & ledger leadFrom CFO office — owns the data spine, the value ledger v0, and attribution rules.
  • 05
    Independent ethics adviserExternal voice on welfare, consent, clinical autonomy. Non-optional.
● PROCESS

Decisions that travel with the journey

The OGSM lesson: process holds when decision rights, cadence and value attribution are explicit from the start.

  • 01
    Decision rights matrixWho decides what at framing, pilot scoping, launch, and scale gates. Signed before scoping starts.
  • 02
    Pre-registered outcomesClinical, commercial and welfare metrics locked before the pilot runs. Prevents result-shopping.
  • 03
    Value ledger (shadow first)Rules for how RC, MVH, Antech, Kinship get credited on a senior-pet pathway. Live by pilot start.
  • 04
    Cadence & gatesWeekly working sessions. Monthly steerco. Go/no-go at scoping end, pilot end, scale decision.
  • 05
    Consent & clinical guardrailsPet-parent consent by design. Vets keep clinical autonomy. Documented and enforced.
● TECH

A minimum viable data stack, not a lake

A defensible v0 of the data stack that is sufficient to trigger action. A unified 360 view follows the value moments rather than precedes them.

  • 01
    Identity resolution layerLink pet across MVH, Royal Canin, Antech, Kinship records. Pet-parent household as anchor.
  • 02
    Minimum viable data spineThe signal set linked: age, breed, weight, history, diagnostics, nutrition, engagement, risk. Live, not perfect.
  • 03
    Decisioning & triggersLightweight rule engine that flags lifecycle moments (senior threshold, diagnostic alert, adherence drop).
  • 04
    Consent managementGranular, purpose-specified consent — re-usable across divisions, designed for pet-parent trust.
  • 05
    Ecosystem LTV dashboardPer-pet contribution view across divisions. Powers the value ledger and the scale decision.

Watch-outs to plan for

These are the failure modes we'd design the enablers against. None are theoretical — each one has played out in adjacent industries when this kind of work loses momentum.

!
Clinical trust comes first

Vets must not feel like cross-sell channels. Clinical judgement leads; decisioning supports. The pilot includes independent ethics review and pre-registered outcomes — these are non-negotiable.

!
The "360 view" trap

Unified data is necessary but not sufficient. Value moments come before data architecture, or the project becomes data plumbing and never produces a result.

!
Pet-parent data sensitivity

Pet data carries emotional weight beyond standard CPG. Consent-by-design is a capability, not an add-on. Plan for it before personalisation gets perceived as surveillance.

!
UK CMA & regulatory backdrop

The March 2026 binding reforms (price transparency, prescription fee caps, ownership disclosure) shape the trust environment. LTV has to be earned through better outcomes, not opaque pricing.

!
Divisional P&L collision

Without the value ledger, one division funds the intervention and another captures the value. The value-ledger enabler addresses this directly.

!
Salesforce & existing tech conversations

Mars has parallel conversations on unified data and "360 parent view." Complement, don't collide. Our framing sits on top: data architecture serves value moments, not the other way around.
